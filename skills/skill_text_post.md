### TTT Social Media Automation | Knowledge Doc

## WHAT IS A TEXT POST?

A post with no image or document — just words. On LinkedIn, well-written text posts often outperform image posts because the algorithm rewards dwell time and comments. On Instagram, text-only posts (via caption on a plain colored graphic or no image) are rare — see Instagram note below.

**Ideal for:** Opinions, personal stories, observations, quick tips, hot takes, industry commentary, milestone announcements, behind-the-scenes thoughts, lessons learned.

---

## PLATFORM RULES

**LinkedIn:** Text-only posts are fully native and highly effective. Use this format for thought leadership, personal brand building, and engagement-driving content.

**Instagram:** Instagram does not support purely text posts. For text-focused content on Instagram:
- Create a simple branded graphic in Canva (plain TTT-colored background + text) → use Single Image skill
- OR post as a Story with text overlay

**Default for text posts: LinkedIn only** unless user specifies otherwise.

## THE TTT TEXT POST STRUCTURE

Every TTT text post follows this architecture:

### THE 5-PART FORMULA

```
PART 1 — THE HOOK (Line 1–2, critical)
  The single most important part. Must stop the scroll.
  LinkedIn shows only the first 2 lines before "...see more"
  Make them NEED to click.

PART 2 — THE TENSION (Lines 3–6)
  Set up the problem, gap, or surprising insight.
  Why does this topic matter RIGHT NOW?

PART 3 — THE VALUE (The bulk of the post)
  Deliver the actual insight, tip, or story.
  Use short paragraphs — max 3 lines each.
  Use line breaks generously. White space = readability.
  Numbered lists or em dashes for structured tips.

PART 4 — THE LANDING (Second to last section)
  Bring it home. What's the core truth or takeaway?
  1–2 punchy lines.

PART 5 — THE CTA (Last line or two)
  One clear action. No more than one ask.
  Examples: "What's your take? Drop it below."
            "Follow TTT for more practical AI builds."
            "DM 'automation' and I'll share the full system."
```

## HOOK FORMULAS (Use One Per Post)

**The Contrarian:**
> "Everyone says X. Here's why that's wrong."
> "Hot take: [conventional wisdom] is holding your business back."

**The Number:**
> "5 things I learned building AI agents for 12 months"
> "3 reasons lean teams are outpacing enterprises in AI adoption"

**The Story Open:**
> "Six months ago, I didn't know what an MCP server was."
> "A client came to me last week with a problem everyone ignores."

**The Bold Statement:**
> "The best AI tool for small teams isn't ChatGPT."
> "Your automation isn't failing because of the tech."

**The Question:**
> "What's the one thing stopping your team from shipping faster?"
> "Are you building AI workflows or just collecting tools?"

**The Curiosity Gap:**
> "There's a 3-step workflow that replaced 4 hours of manual work. Here's what it is:"
> "I automated our entire LinkedIn posting in one afternoon. Here's the exact stack:"

## FORMATTING RULES

**Line length:** Max 8 words per line for impact statements. Regular sentences can be normal length but break into short paragraphs.

**Paragraph size:** 1–3 lines maximum per block. Then a blank line.

**Lists:** Use em dashes (—) or numbers. Not bullet points (LinkedIn doesn't render bullets well on mobile).

**Emojis:** Use sparingly — 1–3 max per post. Only at the start of list items or to highlight a key point. Never mid-sentence. TTT tone is professional first.

**Bold/Italic:** LinkedIn supports bold via Unicode characters (𝗯𝗼𝗹𝗱). Use sparingly for key phrases only — max 2–3 bold phrases per post. Don't bold entire sentences.

**Length:**
- Short post (awareness/engagement): 100–200 words
- Standard post (value/insight): 250–400 words
- Long-form post (story/deep dive): 500–700 words max

---

## HASHTAG RULES

**LinkedIn text posts:** 3–5 hashtags at the end, on their own line.

Selection rules:
- 1 broad hashtag: #AI #Automation #SmallBusiness
- 1–2 mid-range: #AIAgents #WorkflowAutomation #LeanTeams
- 1 TTT-owned: #TeenyTechTrek or #BuildSmallLaunchFast

Never use more than 5 on LinkedIn — it looks spammy and reduces reach.

## STEP-BY-STEP EXECUTION

### Step 1 — Draft the Post
Using the topic provided, write the full post following the 5-part formula and hook formulas above. Apply TTT voice from writing_style.md.

### Step 2 — Self-Review
Read it out loud mentally. Ask:
- Does the first line make me want to read more?
- Is every paragraph earning its place?
- Is the CTA clear and specific?
- Is the formatting clean and readable?
- Is this on-brand for TTT?

Fix anything that feels weak before proceeding.

### Step 3 — Navigate to Platform
Go to linkedin.com/feed. Confirm posting from TTT Company Page.
- Click **Me** dropdown
- If showing personal profile: click **View as** → Switch to TTT Company Page

### Step 4 — Open Post Composer
Click **Start a post** box.

### Step 5 — Paste and Format
Paste the post text. Check that line breaks are preserved. LinkedIn sometimes collapses spacing — manually add line breaks if needed.

Add hashtags on the last line.

### Step 6 — Final Check
Screenshot the composer view. Read through once more. Fix anything.

### Step 7 — Set Visibility and Post
- Visibility: **Anyone** (not "Connections only")
- Click **Post**
- Wait for confirmation that post is live

### Step 8 — Screenshot Confirmation
Take a screenshot of the published post in the feed. Report back to user.
